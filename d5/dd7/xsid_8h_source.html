75 /*
76 Effectively there is only 1 channel, which can either perform Galway Noise
77 or Sampling. However, to achieve all the effects on a C64, 2 sampling
78 channels are required. No divide by 2 is required and is compensated for
79 automatically in the C64 machine code.
80 
81 Confirmed by Warren Pilkington using the tune Turbo Outrun:
82 A new sample must interrupt an existing sample running on the same channel.
83 
84 Confirmed by Michael Schwendt and Antonia Vera using the tune Game Over:
85 A Galway Sample or Noise sequence cannot interrupt any other. However
86 the last of these new requested sequences will be played after the current
87 sequence ends.
88 
89 Lastly playing samples through the SIDs volume is not as clean as playing
90 them on their own channel. Playing through the SID will effect the volume
91 of the other channels and this will be most noticable at low frequencies.
92 These effects are however present in the original SID music.
93 
94 Some SIDs put values directly into the volume register. Others play samples
95 with respect to the current volume. We can't for definate know which the author
96 has chosen originally. We must just make a guess based on what the volume
97 is initially at the start of a sample sequence and from the details xSID has been
98 programmed with.
99 */
